Royal Proclamation

Let it be known across the silent halls of this kingdom that the Queen has heard the words of the fallen knight.

His sword was not raised in pride, but lowered in remorse. And though roses once fell upon the battlefield, their meaning was not lost upon Her Majesty.

After thoughtful deliberation within the chambers of her heart, the Queen has reached her verdict.

The Queen has spoken.

The knight's apology is accepted. His regret has been witnessed, his loyalty restored, and his place within the realm no longer stands in question.

Yet before the knight rides toward the distant lands of Saudi Arabia,

Her Majesty extends a final command.

The Queen would see the knight in person, so that peace may not live merely in words, but in presence.

Thus the decree stands:

The knight is summoned to meet the Queen.

So it is written. So it shall be.
